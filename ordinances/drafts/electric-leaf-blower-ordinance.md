# ORDINANCE NO. ____

## AN ORDINANCE AMENDING THE WEST LINN MUNICIPAL CODE TO RESTRICT THE USE OF GAS-POWERED LEAF BLOWERS WITHIN THE CITY OF WEST LINN, AND DECLARING AN EFFECTIVE DATE

---

### RECITALS

WHEREAS, the City of West Linn has authority under its Charter and under ORS 221.410 and Article XI, Section 2 of the Oregon Constitution to enact ordinances for the protection of public health, safety, and welfare; and

WHEREAS, gas-powered leaf blowers produce noise levels of 80–100+ decibels, significantly exceeding levels associated with hearing damage and neighborhood disturbance; and

WHEREAS, gas-powered leaf blowers emit particulate matter, carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, and volatile organic compounds, contributing to local air pollution and negative health effects; and

WHEREAS, the California Air Resources Board has found that operating a commercial gas-powered leaf blower for one hour produces pollution comparable to driving a passenger vehicle over 1,000 miles; and

WHEREAS, gas-powered leaf blowers disperse particulate matter, allergens, pesticide residue, animal waste, and other debris into the air affecting workers, nearby residents, and passersby; and

WHEREAS, gas-powered leaf blowers can deposit debris into storm drains, stream corridors, and neighboring properties, contributing to water quality degradation; and

WHEREAS, electric leaf blower technology has advanced to provide comparable performance for residential and most commercial applications; and

WHEREAS, the City Council finds that a transition to electric leaf blowers, with a reasonable phase-in period, will reduce noise pollution, reduce airborne dust and particulate exposure, and enhance the quality of life for West Linn residents; and

WHEREAS, this ordinance regulates the use and operation of equipment within the City and is not intended to establish emissions standards, engine certification requirements, or restrictions on the sale or manufacture of any equipment; and

WHEREAS, the West Linn Sustainability Advisory Board has identified the phaseout of gas-powered leaf blowers as a long-term goal for the City; and

WHEREAS, the City of Portland, Oregon has adopted a similar phased restriction on gas-powered leaf blower use, effective 2028, providing regional precedent; and

WHEREAS, this ordinance provides a phase-in period of approximately two years to allow residents and commercial operators to transition equipment;

NOW, THEREFORE, THE CITY OF WEST LINN ORDAINS AS FOLLOWS:

---

### SECTION 1. PURPOSE AND FINDINGS

A. **Purpose.** The purpose of this ordinance is to protect public health, safety, quiet enjoyment, and neighborhood livability by phasing out the use of gas-powered leaf blowers within the City of West Linn, while providing a reasonable transition period for residents and commercial operators. This ordinance regulates the use and operation of equipment and is not intended to regulate emissions standards, engine certification, or product sales.

B. **Findings.** The City Council finds that:

1. Gas-powered leaf blowers generate excessive noise that disturbs the peace, comfort, and repose of residents and interferes with the quiet enjoyment of property;
2. Gas-powered leaf blowers disperse particulate matter, allergens, dust, and debris into the air, affecting the health of operators, nearby residents, workers, and passersby;
3. Gas-powered leaf blowers deposit debris into storm drains, stream corridors, and neighboring properties;
4. Electric-powered leaf blowers produce significantly lower noise levels and avoid the nuisance and public health impacts of gas-powered equipment;
5. A transition period of approximately two years provides sufficient time for residents and commercial operators to acquire electric equipment;
6. This ordinance is a reasonable exercise of the City's police power to regulate noise, nuisance, and public health, and is not preempted by state or federal law.

---

### SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS

For purposes of this ordinance:

A. **"Gas-powered leaf blower"** means any portable, hand-held or backpack-mounted device that is powered by a gasoline engine or other internal combustion engine and is designed or used to blow leaves, dirt, debris, or other materials.

B. **"Electric leaf blower"** means any portable, hand-held or backpack-mounted device that is powered by an electric motor, whether corded or battery-operated, and is designed or used to blow leaves, dirt, debris, or other materials.

C. **"Commercial operator"** means any person or business entity that uses a leaf blower in connection with a commercial landscaping, yard maintenance, or property management business, whether or not for compensation.

D. **"Residential property"** means any property zoned or used primarily for residential purposes.

E. **"Person"** means any individual, firm, partnership, corporation, association, or other legal entity.

---

### SECTION 3. PROHIBITION ON GAS-POWERED LEAF BLOWERS

A. **Effective January 1, 2028,** no person shall use, or cause to be used, a gas-powered leaf blower within the City of West Linn.

B. **Owner responsibility.** No owner or person in control of real property shall permit a contractor, landscaper, employee, tenant, or agent to use a gas-powered leaf blower on property within that person's control.

C. This prohibition applies to all properties within City limits, including but not limited to residential, commercial, industrial, and public properties.

---

### SECTION 3A. GENERAL OPERATING STANDARDS (ALL LEAF BLOWERS)

Regardless of power source, no person may operate any leaf blower in a manner that:

A. Deposits dust, debris, or other material onto neighboring property, a public sidewalk, street, storm drain, stream corridor, or park;

B. Unreasonably disturbs the peace, comfort, or repose of nearby occupants;

C. Is inconsistent with any otherwise applicable City noise or nuisance provisions.

---

### SECTION 4. EXEMPTIONS

The prohibition in Section 3 shall not apply to:

A. **Emergency operations.** Use of gas-powered leaf blowers by City personnel, public utilities, or their contractors in response to a declared emergency, storm event, or natural disaster, when electric equipment is insufficient or unavailable.

B. **City operations during transition.** Use by the City of West Linn or its contractors for public property maintenance through June 30, 2029, to allow the City to complete its own equipment transition.

C. **Fire hazard mitigation.** Use of gas-powered leaf blowers when required for fire prevention or fuel reduction activities directed or approved by the Fire Marshal or Tualatin Valley Fire & Rescue.

D. **Agricultural operations.** Use on properties actively engaged in commercial agricultural operations, as defined by ORS 215.203.

E. **Temporary hardship waiver.** The City Manager or designee may grant a temporary written waiver, not to exceed 180 days, to any person who demonstrates:
   1. That compliance requires substantial electrical service upgrades, equipment procurement delays outside the applicant's control, or other documented hardship;
   2. That the applicant has made a good-faith effort to transition to electric equipment.

   A hardship waiver may be conditioned on mitigation measures (e.g., restricted hours, muffler requirements) and may be renewed once for an additional period not to exceed 180 days upon good cause shown.

---

### SECTION 5. HOURS OF OPERATION FOR ELECTRIC LEAF BLOWERS

To minimize noise disturbance, the use of any leaf blower (including electric) shall be subject to the following time restrictions:

A. **Residential zones:**
   - Weekdays (Monday–Friday): 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM
   - Weekends and federal holidays: 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM

B. **Commercial and industrial zones:**
   - Monday–Saturday: 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM
   - Sundays and federal holidays: 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM

C. **Noise standard.** No electric leaf blower shall produce noise exceeding 65 decibels measured at a distance of 50 feet from the source during operation in a residential zone.

---

### SECTION 6. ENFORCEMENT

A. **Violations.** A violation of this ordinance is a Class 1 civil infraction.

B. **Penalties.**
   1. First violation: Written warning.
   2. Second violation within 12 months: Fine of $100.
   3. Third and subsequent violations within 12 months: Fine of $250.

C. **Commercial operator penalties.** For commercial operators:
   1. First violation: Fine of $250.
   2. Second violation within 12 months: Fine of $500.
   3. Third and subsequent violations within 12 months: Fine of $1,000 and suspension of the operator's City business license for 30 days.

D. **Enforcement authority.** This ordinance shall be enforced by the West Linn Police Department and/or the City's Code Enforcement division. Enforcement may be initiated based on complaint.

E. **Each day a separate violation.** Each day on which a violation occurs or continues constitutes a separate violation. Each distinct property at which a violation occurs may constitute a separate violation.

F. **Evidence.** The City may rely on officer observation, code-enforcement investigation, witness statements, photographs, video, or other reasonably reliable evidence.

---

### SECTION 6A. APPEALS

Any person issued a civil penalty under this ordinance may appeal to the City's hearings officer within 10 business days of service of the notice of violation.

---

### SECTION 7. EDUCATION AND OUTREACH

A. The City shall develop and implement a public education program beginning no later than July 1, 2027, to inform residents and commercial operators of the requirements of this ordinance.

B. The education program shall include:
   1. Information on the health, environmental, and noise-reduction benefits of electric leaf blowers;
   2. Resources for identifying and purchasing electric leaf blower equipment;
   3. Information regarding available rebate programs, if any;
   4. Notice to commercial landscaping businesses operating within City limits.

C. The City Council may, by resolution, establish a rebate or incentive program to assist residents and commercial operators in transitioning to electric equipment, subject to budget appropriation.

---

### SECTION 8. SEVERABILITY

If any section, subsection, sentence, clause, phrase, or portion of this ordinance is for any reason held to be invalid or unconstitutional by a court of competent jurisdiction, such portion shall be deemed a separate, distinct, and independent provision, and such holding shall not affect the validity of the remaining portions of this ordinance.

---

### SECTION 9. CODIFICATION

The City Recorder is directed to codify this ordinance in the appropriate title and chapter of the West Linn Municipal Code.

---

### SECTION 10. EFFECTIVE DATE

This ordinance shall take effect 30 days after its adoption, in accordance with Section [___] of the West Linn City Charter. The prohibition on gas-powered leaf blowers in Section 3 shall take effect on January 1, 2028.

## LEGAL ANALYSIS NOTES (Internal — Not Part of Ordinance)

### Authority
- ORS 221.410 — Power of city to control local affairs
- Oregon Constitution Article XI, §2 — Home rule
- Consistent with existing West Linn noise ordinance authority
- No state preemption on equipment type restrictions

### State Law Consistency
- ✅ No ORS preemption on leaf blower regulation
- ✅ ORS 192.640 — Proper notice and hearing procedures required for adoption
- ✅ ORS 221.310 — Ordinance effective date procedures
- ✅ Does not conflict with DEQ authority (ORS 468) — local regulation can be more restrictive
- ✅ Agricultural exemption aligns with ORS 215.203 definitions

### Federal Law Consistency
- ✅ No EPA preemption — ordinance is framed as USE regulation (noise/nuisance/public health), NOT emissions standard
- ✅ Explicitly states: "not intended to regulate emissions standards, engine certification, or product sales"
- ✅ Does NOT ban sales of gas leaf blowers — only bans USE within city limits
- ✅ CAA §209(e) preempts local emissions standards for nonroad engines, but EPA recognizes cities may regulate USE and OPERATION of nonroad engines
- ✅ No takings issue — rational basis review; no property right to specific equipment
- ✅ Equal protection — applies uniformly to all users
- ✅ No First Amendment issue — regulates conduct, not speech
- ✅ Commerce Clause — regulates USE within city, not sale or manufacture
- ✅ Portland OR has adopted identical approach (property-owner enforcement, phased timeline to 2028)
- ✅ 100+ California cities have sustained similar bans without successful CAA challenges

### Potential Challenges & Mitigations
1. **CAA preemption claim** → Mitigated by explicit "use regulation, not emissions standard" framing throughout; does not ban sales; does not reference engine certification; follows Portland's tested model
2. **Commercial landscaper pushback** → Mitigated by 2-year phase-in, 180-day hardship waiver (renewable once), potential rebate program
3. **"Too aggressive" claims** → Jan 2028 gives ~2 years from adoption; matches Portland's identical timeline; West Linn SAB already supports this direction
4. **Enforcement burden** → Complaint-based enforcement minimizes resource impact; warning-first approach for residents
5. **Battery technology concerns** → Exemption for emergencies; technology adequate for most uses as of 2026; hardship waiver covers edge cases
6. **Owner liability concerns** → Follows Portland model; reasonable — property owners control who works on their property

### Key Framing Principle
⚠️ **NEVER frame this as an emissions regulation.** Always frame as:
- Noise reduction
- Nuisance abatement
- Public health (dust, particulates, debris — not tailpipe emissions)
- Quiet enjoyment of property
- Storm drain / water quality protection
- Worker health (dust exposure)

This distinction is the difference between a defensible ordinance and one that gets struck down on federal preemption.

### Comparable Jurisdictions
- Portland, OR — explored similar restrictions
- Washington, DC — banned gas leaf blowers effective Jan 2025
- 100+ California cities — various gas leaf blower bans
- Numerous cities in WA, CO, NY, NJ have enacted similar bans

### Recommended Process
1. First reading at council meeting
2. Refer to public hearing (ORS 192.640 notice requirements)
3. Public comment period (consider 30-day written comment period)
4. Second reading and adoption
5. Begin education/outreach program (Section 7)
6. Prohibition takes effect January 1, 2028
