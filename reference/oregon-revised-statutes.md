## FIREARMS (FULL STATE PREEMPTION)

### ORS 166.170 — State Preemption of Local Firearms Regulation
**West Linn CANNOT enact any ordinance regulating:**
- Sale, acquisition, transfer, ownership, possession, storage, transportation, or use of firearms
- Any tax on firearms or ammunition beyond general sales tax
- Registration of firearms

**Exception:** Cities can regulate discharge of firearms within city limits and possession in public buildings.

### ORS 166.173 — Authority of city to regulate firearms in public buildings
- Must provide lockers/storage for lawful firearms carriers
- Must post notice

## CANNABIS

### ORS 475C — Cannabis Regulation
- **475C.305–475C.313** — Homegrown cannabis (personal use): cities CANNOT ban personal cultivation within state limits
- **475C.097** — Retail licensing
- **475C.101** — Proximity of retailer to school (1,000 feet)
- **475C.113** — Local delivery ordinances
- **475C.377** — Prohibition on public use
- **475C.811–475C.821** — **Local opt-out for retail sales** — City or county may prohibit establishment of retail sales by referring measure to voters

### West Linn can:
- Regulate time, place, manner of cannabis businesses (within state framework)
- Opt out of retail sales via voter referendum
- Regulate public consumption

### West Linn cannot:
- Ban personal home cultivation (up to 4 plants per household)
- Override state licensing standards
- Impose local cannabis taxes (prohibited by ORS 475C.113)

## WEST LINN-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

### Population Triggers
West Linn (~27,000) triggers these state requirements:
- **HB 2001 middle housing** — Must allow duplexes on all residential lots; triplexes, quadplexes, townhomes in some zones (cities >25,000)
- **HB 2003 housing production strategy** — Required
- **Predictive scheduling (ORS 653.412+)** — Applies to large employers in West Linn, not the city itself as regulator
- **Portland metro minimum wage tier** — Highest state rate applies

### Metro Regional Government
As a city within Metro's boundary, West Linn must comply with:
- Metro Urban Growth Management Functional Plan (Titles 1–14)
- Title 1: Housing capacity requirements
- Title 3: Water quality, flood management, fish/wildlife habitat
- Title 4: Industrial/employment land protection
- Title 7: Affordable housing
- Title 11: Planning for areas added to UGB
- Title 13: Nature in Neighborhoods (habitat protection)
- Metro Transportation Plan
- Regional waste management policies

### Clackamas County
- County provides some services (health, courts, assessment)
- Urban services agreement between West Linn and county
- County comprehensive plan applies to unincorporated areas near West Linn
