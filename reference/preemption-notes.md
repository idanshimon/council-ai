# Oregon State Preemption Areas — West Linn Reference

**Preemption** = state law prohibits or limits local ordinances in that area.
This is the FIRST thing to check before drafting any ordinance.

---

## 🔴 FULL PREEMPTION (Cannot Legislate Locally)

### Firearms — ORS 166.170
State **fully preempts** local firearms regulation. West Linn CANNOT:
- Regulate sale, acquisition, transfer, ownership, possession, storage, or transportation of firearms
- Tax firearms or ammunition (beyond general taxes)
- Require registration

**Exceptions allowed:**
- Regulate discharge of firearms within city limits
- Regulate possession in public buildings (must provide lockers, post notice — ORS 166.173)

### Minimum Wage — ORS 653.017
West Linn cannot set a local minimum wage. State sets three tiers:
- Portland metro (includes West Linn): highest tier
- Standard counties
- Nonurban counties

### Rent Control — ORS 91.225
Cities **cannot enact local rent control**. State controls via SB 608 / ORS 90.323:
- Max rent increase: 7% + CPI per year (with exceptions for new construction <15 years old)
- West Linn can encourage affordable housing via other tools (inclusionary zoning, incentives) but cannot cap rents

### Predictive Scheduling — ORS 653.490
State preempts local work scheduling ordinances. West Linn cannot create local scheduling requirements beyond state law.

### Sick Leave — ORS 653.661
State preempts local sick leave ordinances. State law (ORS 653.601–653.656) sets the standard.

### Cannabis Taxation — ORS 475C.113
Cities cannot impose local taxes on cannabis. Revenue sharing is set at state level.

## 🟡 PARTIAL PREEMPTION (Limited Local Authority)

### Land Use & Zoning — ORS 197, 227
Oregon's statewide planning goals constrain local zoning. West Linn must:
- Have a comprehensive plan consistent with 19 statewide planning goals
- Zone consistent with comprehensive plan (ORS 197.250)
- Allow "needed housing" types (ORS 197.303)
- Use clear and objective standards for housing (ORS 197.307)
- Cannot prohibit ADUs (ORS 197.312)
- **Must allow middle housing** (HB 2001 / ORS 197.758+): duplexes on all residential lots; triplexes, quadplexes, townhomes in some zones (West Linn pop >25,000)
- Subject to LUBA appeal for inconsistent land use decisions

**What West Linn CAN do:**
- Set design standards (height, setbacks, lot coverage) within reason
- Require tree preservation, environmental protections
- Establish overlay zones (historic, environmental, etc.)
- Regulate commercial and industrial uses (within comp plan)

### Cannabis — ORS 475C
**Can:** Opt out of retail sales via voter referendum; regulate time/place/manner of cannabis businesses; regulate public consumption
**Cannot:** Ban personal home cultivation (up to 4 plants); override state licensing; impose local cannabis taxes

### Housing — ORS 90, 91, 197
State increasingly controls housing policy:
- Cannot prohibit needed housing types
- Cannot impose excessive conditions on housing development
- Must comply with housing needs analysis (ORS 197.296)
- HB 2003 requires housing production strategy
- Inclusionary zoning allowed with limits (ORS 197.309)

### Employment — ORS 653
- Cannot set wages, scheduling, or sick leave beyond state law
- Can set employment standards for city employees (within PECBA — ORS 243)
- Prevailing wage on public contracts (ORS 279C.800+) is state-controlled

### Public Meetings — ORS 192.610–192.710
State law dictates meeting procedures. West Linn must:
- Give proper notice for all meetings
- Keep minutes
- Only enter executive session for reasons listed in ORS 192.660
- **Email chains between quorum of councilors on city business = public meeting** (ORS 192.695)
- Violations can void council actions

### Budget — ORS 294
State Local Budget Law governs entire budget process. West Linn must follow all procedures for budget committee, hearings, publication, adoption.

## 🟢 LOCAL AUTHORITY (Can Legislate Freely)

### General Police Power
- Noise ordinances
- Nuisance abatement
- **Equipment use restrictions** (leaf blowers, lawn mowers, etc.) — MUST frame as noise/nuisance/health, NOT as emissions regulation (see federal CAA §209(e) preemption in federal-frameworks.md)
- Animal control
- Park rules and regulations
- Business licensing (general)
- Sign regulations (must be content-neutral per *Reed v. Town of Gilbert*)

### Traffic & Parking
- Speed limits on city streets (ORS 811.111)
- Parking regulations
- Street design standards

### Building & Development
- Building codes (must adopt state codes — ORS 455 — but can add local amendments within limits)
- Stormwater management
- Utility connections
- SDCs / impact fees (within ORS 223.297–223.314 framework)

### Public Safety
- Police department operations
- Emergency management
- Code enforcement

### Utilities & Franchises
- Franchise agreements with utilities
- Water/sewer rates and service
- Stormwater utility fees

### Local Governance
- Council rules of procedure
- Committee/commission establishment
- Intergovernmental agreements
- City manager authority and duties

## ⚠️ FEDERAL PREEMPTION (Also Applies)

### Constitutional Limits
- **1st Amendment:** Sign ordinances must be content-neutral (*Reed v. Town of Gilbert*). Public forum doctrine limits speech regulation in public spaces. RLUIPA protects religious land use.
- **2nd Amendment:** *Bruen* (2022) test applies, though Oregon state preemption handles most local issues.
- **5th/14th Amendment:** Regulatory takings. *Nollan/Dolan* — development conditions must have nexus and rough proportionality.
- **Fair Housing Act:** Cannot enact discriminatory housing ordinances. Source-of-income protections.
- **ADA:** Public facilities must be accessible.
- **Clean Water Act / Clean Air Act:** Environmental permits.
- **FLSA:** Federal labor floor (Oregon's is higher, so state controls in practice).

## Checking for Preemption — Quick Process

When analyzing a proposed ordinance:

1. **What's the subject area?** (housing, firearms, employment, land use, etc.)
2. **Check this file** — is it in 🔴, 🟡, or 🟢?
3. **If 🔴** — STOP. Cannot legislate. Advise council.
4. **If 🟡** — Research specific ORS sections. What exactly can the city do within state limits?
5. **If 🟢** — Check federal constitutional limits. Draft with confidence.
6. **Always:** Search for recent legislative changes. Oregon legislature meets in odd years (long session) and even years (short session). Bills can change preemption status.

### Portland Precedent (Neighboring City)
Portland has adopted a phased gas leaf blower ban:
- Regulates property owners allowing gas leaf blowers on property
- Permits electric leaf blowers year-round
- Includes extension if electrical upgrades needed
- Escalating civil penalties
- Full gas blower ban reaches effect in 2028
- Portland City Code Title 18 (Noise Control) — 55 dBA residential baseline
- West Linn can cite Portland's approach as regional precedent
