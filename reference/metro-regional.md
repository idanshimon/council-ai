# Metro Regional Government — West Linn Compliance Reference

West Linn is within the Portland Metro urban growth boundary and must comply with Metro's regional framework.

**Metro website:** https://www.oregonmetro.gov
**Metro Code:** https://www.oregonmetro.gov/metro-code

---

## Metro Urban Growth Management Functional Plan

The Functional Plan implements the 2040 Growth Concept. Titles that bind West Linn:

### Title 1 — Housing Capacity
- West Linn must maintain sufficient zoned capacity for its share of regional housing need
- Minimum density requirements in some areas
- Cannot downzone below capacity targets without Metro approval

### Title 3 — Water Quality, Flood Management, Fish & Wildlife Habitat
- Protections for streams, floodplains, wetlands
- Vegetated corridors required along waterways
- Affects what West Linn can permit near Willamette River, Tualatin River, tributaries
- Erosion control requirements

### Title 4 — Industrial and Other Employment Areas
- Protects employment land from conversion to residential/commercial
- Limits what West Linn can rezone

### Title 7 — Housing Choice
- Affordable housing strategies
- West Linn must demonstrate compliance with regional housing equity goals

### Title 8 — Compliance Procedures
- How Metro enforces functional plan requirements
- Can require plan amendments if city is out of compliance

### Title 11 — Planning for Areas Added to UGB
- If urban growth boundary expands near West Linn, specific planning requirements apply

### Title 12 — Protection of Residential Neighborhoods
- Protections against large retail in residential areas

### Title 13 — Nature in Neighborhoods
- Habitat protection requirements
- Significant natural resource areas (mapped)
- West Linn must have a program to protect habitat

### Title 14 — Urban Growth Boundary
- UGB management procedures

## Transportation

- Metro coordinates regional transportation planning
- Regional Transportation Plan (RTP) affects local transportation projects
- West Linn must have a Transportation System Plan (TSP) consistent with RTP
- TriMet provides transit service

---

## Regional Parks & Open Spaces
- Metro owns/manages regional parks and natural areas
- Bond measures fund land acquisition
- Coordination on trails (e.g., Trolley Trail, Willamette River connections)

---

## Solid Waste
- Metro has authority over regional solid waste management
- Franchise and flow control
- Recycling requirements

---

_Cross-reference Metro requirements when drafting ordinances touching land use, housing, environmental protection, or transportation._
