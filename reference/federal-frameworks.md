# Federal Law Frameworks Affecting West Linn Ordinances

---

## Constitutional Limits on Local Government

### First Amendment
- **Sign ordinances:** Must be content-neutral after *Reed v. Town of Gilbert* (2015). Cannot regulate signs based on message (e.g., treating political signs differently from real estate signs).
- **Public forum doctrine:** Cannot ban speech in traditional public forums (parks, sidewalks). Can impose reasonable time/place/manner restrictions.
- **Religious land use (RLUIPA):** Cannot impose land use regulations that substantially burden religious exercise without compelling interest. Cannot discriminate against or exclude religious assemblies.
- **Assembly:** Cannot require permits with unfettered discretion over approval.

### Second Amendment
- **NYSPRA v. Bruen (2022):** Firearms regulations must be consistent with historical tradition. Mostly academic for West Linn since Oregon fully preempts local firearms regulation.

### Fifth Amendment / Fourteenth Amendment — Takings & Due Process
- **Regulatory takings:** If an ordinance goes "too far" in restricting property use, it can be a taking requiring compensation (*Penn Central* balancing test, *Lucas* total taking).
- **Nollan v. California Coastal Commission (1987):** Conditions on development permits must have an "essential nexus" to a legitimate government interest.
- **Dolan v. City of Tigard (1994):** Conditions must have "rough proportionality" to the impact of the development. *Note: Dolan was an Oregon case from neighboring Tigard.*
- **Koontz v. St. Johns River (2013):** Nollan/Dolan applies to monetary exactions too.
- **Procedural due process:** Must provide notice and opportunity to be heard before depriving someone of property rights (land use denials, code enforcement).

### Fourteenth Amendment — Equal Protection
- Cannot classify based on race, national origin (strict scrutiny), gender (intermediate scrutiny), or irrationally target any group.
- Zoning that has disparate impact on protected classes can violate Fair Housing Act.

## Federal Statutes

### Fair Housing Act (42 USC §3601–3619)
- Prohibits discrimination in housing based on race, color, religion, sex, familial status, national origin, disability.
- **Disparate impact:** An ordinance neutral on its face can violate FHA if it disproportionately affects a protected class (*Texas Dept. of Housing v. Inclusive Communities*, 2015).
- **Reasonable accommodations:** City must make reasonable accommodations in zoning/land use for persons with disabilities (e.g., group homes).
- **Source-of-income:** Not federally required, but Oregon state law (ORS 659A.421) prohibits discrimination based on source of income (Section 8 vouchers).

### Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- **Title II:** City programs, services, activities must be accessible.
- Public facilities, sidewalks, parks must meet ADA standards.
- City website accessibility (increasing litigation area).
- Zoning cannot exclude group homes for disabled persons.

### Clean Water Act (33 USC §1251+)
- NPDES permits for stormwater discharge (West Linn has MS4 permit obligations).
- Cannot adopt ordinances less protective than CWA requirements.
- Affects development standards near waterways, riparian areas.

### Clean Air Act (42 USC §7401+) — ⚠️ CRITICAL FOR EQUIPMENT BANS
- DEQ administers in Oregon. Mostly affects industrial permitting.
- Wood stove regulations (Oregon DEQ has state rules).
- **Section 209(e) — Nonroad Engine Preemption:** Federal law preempts states and cities from adopting their own *emissions standards* for many nonroad engines (including lawn equipment engines). This means:
  - ❌ West Linn CANNOT frame an equipment ban as an emissions regulation
  - ❌ Cannot set local emission limits for small engines
  - ❌ Cannot require engine certification to a local standard
  - ✅ CAN regulate the *use and operation* of equipment (noise, nuisance, hours, location)
  - ✅ CAN ban use of gas-powered equipment as a noise/nuisance/public health measure
  - ✅ CAN regulate property owners allowing certain equipment on their property
- **Key framing rule:** Any ordinance restricting gas-powered equipment (leaf blowers, mowers, etc.) MUST be framed as a use/operation/nuisance regulation, NOT as an emissions standard. The ordinance should explicitly state it does not regulate emissions standards, engine certification, or product sales.
- **Precedent:** 100+ California cities, Washington DC, Portland OR have enacted gas leaf blower bans framed as use restrictions — none have been successfully challenged on CAA preemption grounds.

### Endangered Species Act (16 USC §1531+)
- Affects development near habitat of listed species.
- Willamette River salmon/steelhead habitat relevant to West Linn.
- City ordinances can be more protective but not less.

### National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA)
- Applies only to federal actions/funding. If West Linn uses federal funds (CDBG, federal transportation $), NEPA review may be required.

### HUD / Community Development Block Grants (CDBG)
- If West Linn receives CDBG or other HUD funds: additional fair housing, environmental review, and reporting requirements.
- Affirmatively Furthering Fair Housing (AFFH) obligations.

### Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)
- National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) — West Linn must maintain compliant floodplain ordinances to participate.
- Flood Insurance Rate Maps (FIRMs) affect development in flood zones.
- Cannot adopt less restrictive floodplain standards than FEMA minimums.

### Telecommunications Act of 1996
- **Section 253:** Cities cannot prohibit provision of telecom services.
- **Section 332(c)(7):** Limits on local regulation of cell tower siting. Cannot unreasonably discriminate, must act within reasonable time, denial must be in writing with substantial evidence.
- **FCC Small Cell Order (2018):** Limits fees and aesthetic requirements for small cell facilities.

### Religious Land Use and Institutionalized Persons Act (RLUIPA)
- Substantial burden test for land use regulations affecting religious institutions.
- Equal treatment provision — cannot treat religious assemblies worse than secular assemblies.
- West Linn cannot zone out churches from commercial or residential areas if secular assembly uses are permitted.

## Key Federal Cases Relevant to Oregon Cities

| Case | Year | Relevance |
|------|------|-----------|
| *Reed v. Town of Gilbert* | 2015 | Sign ordinances must be content-neutral |
| *Dolan v. City of Tigard* | 1994 | Development conditions — rough proportionality (Oregon case!) |
| *Nollan v. CA Coastal Comm.* | 1987 | Development conditions — essential nexus |
| *Koontz v. St. Johns River* | 2013 | Nollan/Dolan applies to monetary exactions |
| *Penn Central v. NYC* | 1978 | Regulatory takings balancing test |
| *Lucas v. SC Coastal Council* | 1992 | Total takings — must compensate |
| *TX Dept. Housing v. Inclusive Communities* | 2015 | Fair Housing Act disparate impact |
| *NYSPRA v. Bruen* | 2022 | Second Amendment — historical tradition test |
| *City of Grants Pass v. Johnson* | 2024 | Cities can enforce camping bans (overturns 9th Circuit *Martin v. Boise* in part) |
